  • About Gandhi
    • Mahatma Gandhi (born October 2, 1869, Porbandar, India—died January 30, 1948, Delhi) was an Indian lawyer, politician, social activist, and writer who became the leader of the nationalist movement against the British rule of India. As such, he came to be considered the father of his country.

Year Event
1869 Birth in Porbandar, India
1893 Moved to South Africa to work and began his activism
1915 Returned to India and began leading the independence movement
1930 Led the Salt March to protest British salt tax
1947 India gained independence from British rule
1948 Assassinated by Nathuram Godse
Award/Title Year Description
Time's Person of the Year 1930 Named Person of the Year by Time magazine for his role in the Salt March and his nonviolent resistance movement.
Freedom Fighter Posthumously Recognized Considered one of the most important freedom fighters of the 20th century for his role in India’s independence.
Father of the Nation Posthumously Recognized Honored as the Father of the Nation in India due to his crucial role in achieving Indian independence.
Quote Context
“Be the change that you wish to see in the world.” Encourages individuals to take personal responsibility for creating positive change.
“The best way to find yourself is to lose yourself in the service of others.” Highlights the importance of selfless service as a means of personal growth.
“Live as if you were to die tomorrow. Learn as if you were to live forever.” Emphasizes the importance of living fully while continuously seeking knowledge.
